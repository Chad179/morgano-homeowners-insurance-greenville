How does the SC wind hail deductible work?

Think of it as a second, bigger deductible that only wakes up for wind, hail, or named storms, separate from the all-perils deductible on the rest of your policy. It is usually written as a percentage of your dwelling coverage, 1 to 5 percent, though a few carriers we use in the Upstate still offer a flat dollar version. Here is what that means on the ground in Greenville: a $300,000 home off Wade Hampton Boulevard with a 2 percent wind and hail deductible covers the first $6,000 of any storm claim itself before the carrier pays a dime. The percentage and how it applies are set in each carrier's filing with the SC Department of Insurance.

What is the wind hail deductible for homeowners insurance in Greenville SC?

Greenville homeowners usually land somewhere between a flat $1,000 to $5,000 wind and hail deductible, which some Upstate carriers still write, and 1 to 2 percent of dwelling value, which is what most carriers default to here. We sit well inland in Greenville County, so we get the gentler end of the scale. The coastal counties, Charleston, Beaufort, and Horry down around Myrtle Beach, carry higher mandatory percentages, typically 2 to 5 percent, because of hurricane exposure the Upstate simply does not have. When we quote a home near Paris Mountain or out in Simpsonville, comparing those deductible structures across carriers is the first thing we do, because the cheapest headline premium often hides a 5 percent storm deductible.

Can I lower the wind hail deductible in SC?

In most cases, yes. Almost every South Carolina carrier lets you pick the wind and hail deductible when the policy is issued, and we walk Greenville clients through the trade-off every time. Dropping from 5 percent down to 1 percent usually adds 8 to 15 percent to the annual premium, and a few Upstate carriers will write a flat $1,000 or $2,500 wind and hail deductible here for a premium add-on of roughly 10 to 20 percent. The one place your hands are tied is the coast: those counties cannot go below the mandatory percentage minimums carriers file with the SC Department of Insurance. We help you weigh the higher yearly cost against what you could actually absorb after a storm.

Does the wind hail deductible apply to all wind damage in SC?

Generally it applies to any wind or hail loss, which in the Upstate means the things we actually see: a pine coming down across a roof during a Greenville County thunderstorm, hail dents after a spring line of storms rolls through Greer, straight-line wind, and tornado damage. Some carriers split it further with a separate named-storm deductible that only triggers for an officially named hurricane or tropical storm, leaving everyday wind on the lower standard deductible. We get a lot of remnant-storm wind up here rather than direct hurricanes, so that distinction matters for our clients. Your declarations page spells out exactly which structure your policy uses, and we read it with you before you bind.

Is roof damage subject to the SC wind hail deductible?

Yes, and this is the conversation I least like having after a storm. Roof wind and hail damage falls under the separate wind and hail deductible, and on top of that many SC carriers settle roofs older than 10 to 15 years at Actual Cash Value, meaning they pay the depreciated value rather than full replacement cost. Put those together on a real Greenville home: a 20-year-old roof with hail damage on a $300,000 house with a 2 percent deductible can net the homeowner only 30 to 50 percent of what a new roof costs once both the deductible and the ACV depreciation come out. That is why, when we review an older home around Cherrydale or Taylors, we push to keep replacement-cost roof coverage in place where the carrier still offers it. Each carrier's roof-settlement rules are filed with the SC Department of Insurance.
